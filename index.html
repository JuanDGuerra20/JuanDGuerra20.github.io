About Me

I am a first-year M.ASc student at Polytechnique Montreal and Mila - Quebec AI Institute, supervised by Professor Marco Bonizzato, with co-supervisors Professors Guillaume Lajoie, Numa Dancause, and Lama Seoud.

Born in Quito, Ecuador, I moved to Toronto during childhood and later to Montreal in 2020 for university studies. My passion lies in employing Machine Learning in healthcare, especially in Neuroscience applications.

I completed my Honours B.Sc in Computer Science at McGill University with a minor in Neuroscience, graduating with First Class Honours. My research experiences span neuroprosthetics, LLM hallucinations, fMRI & TMS analysis through ML interpretability, and computational neuroscience.

Research Interests

Neuroprosthetics

Developing Machine Learning pipelines and architectures to solve neurostimulation problems for traumatic spinal cord injury therapy.

Machine Learning

Designing AI algorithms with applications in healthcare, focusing on interpretability and reliability in critical domains.

LLM Safety

Researching methods to detect and mitigate hallucinations in Large Language Models for safer AI deployment.

Neuroimaging

Applying machine learning interpretability techniques to fMRI and TMS research for understanding brain function.
